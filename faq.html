FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

How AutoDoc works, what it does on your machine, and what to expect at launch.

What is AutoDoc?

AutoDoc is a desktop app that records your meetings, transcribes them, identifies who's speaking, and turns them into structured notes — all running on your own computer. It also lets you search across every meeting and ask questions about them in a chat. The transcription and AI note-taking happen on-device, so your recordings, transcripts, and notes don't leave your machine.

How is it different from typical cloud meeting tools?

Most meeting assistants send a bot into your call and process and store everything on their servers. AutoDoc does neither. It captures audio directly on your device — no bot ever joins the meeting — and runs transcription and note-taking with local AI models. Your data stays on your disk, encrypted, and the app works offline. It's also open source under the AGPL-3.0 license.

Which meeting apps does it work with?

Because AutoDoc captures audio from your device rather than integrating with each service, it works with virtually any meeting software. It can automatically detect calls in Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, Slack, and Discord and offer to start notes for you.

Does a bot join my meeting?

No. AutoDoc records system audio and your microphone locally, the same way screen recording works. Nothing appears in the participant list, so there's no "recording bot" announcing itself in the call. As always, make sure you have consent to record according to your local laws and company policy.

Where is my data stored?

Everything is stored locally on your Mac, under ~/Library/Application Support/AutoDoc/. Recordings, transcripts, and notes live there in encrypted form and are never uploaded to a server by AutoDoc.

How does the on-device AI work?

Transcription runs locally using an open Whisper speech-to-text model (via whisper.cpp, with acceleration on Apple Silicon). Structured notes and the Ask AI chat run on a local large language model through a bundled Ollama runtime. AutoDoc downloads the AI models it needs on first run; after that, transcription and note-taking can run completely offline. No audio or transcript is sent to the cloud for AI processing.

What's the difference between Search and Ask AI?

Search is keyword lookup: type a phrase and AutoDoc finds every transcript and note that matches, then jumps you to the exact moment it was said.

Ask AI is conversational: ask a question in plain English (like "what did we decide about the budget?") and it answers in a chat, grounded in your transcripts and notes across meetings.

What calendars does it connect to, and what can it see?

AutoDoc can connect to Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook. Connecting is optional. When connected, it reads your upcoming events so it can name recordings, match meetings to calendar entries, suggest attendee names for speakers, and offer per-meeting auto-record. Calendar tokens are encrypted and stored locally in your system keychain.

How is my data encrypted?

Recordings, transcripts, notes, and metadata are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. The encryption key is held in your macOS Keychain via the operating system's secure storage, so your meeting library is protected on disk.

What are the system requirements?

AutoDoc currently runs on macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. Running AI models locally is more demanding than a typical app — a recent Mac with enough free disk space for the AI models (a few gigabytes, downloaded on first run) is recommended, and Apple Silicon gives the best transcription performance.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Once the AI models are downloaded, recording, transcription, note-taking, search, and Ask AI all run without an internet connection. The app only needs the network for optional things like syncing your calendar, downloading models, and checking for updates.

Do you collect any analytics?

Only if you opt in. During setup you're asked whether to share anonymous usage and crash diagnostics; it's off unless you choose to enable it. Even then, your meeting content is never part of that — diagnostics are limited to lightweight events and crash reports.

Is it open source? How much does it cost?

AutoDoc is open source under the AGPL-3.0 license. It's free to use during early access. We may introduce paid plans down the road, but the goal right now is to get it into the hands of people who care about private, local-first meeting notes.

When can I get it, and what platforms are supported?

AutoDoc is launching on macOS first, with a Windows version in development. Early access is opening soon — downloads and the public source repository will be linked here when they're ready.
